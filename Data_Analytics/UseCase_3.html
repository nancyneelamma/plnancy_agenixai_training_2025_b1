# Convert attribute target from numerical values into categorical values

def insulationTypes(insulationtype):
    if insulationtype == 'Poor':
        return 0
    elif insulationtype == 'Fair':
        return 1
    elif insulationtype == 'Good':
        return 2
    elif insulationtype == 'Excellent':
        return 3

# Add them to the dataframe

energy_data['InsulationType_Category'] = energy_data['InsulationType'].apply(insulationTypes)

REPORT¶

Summary¶

This report analyzes how buildings consume energy and what factors influence their energy use. It looks at building size, insulation, renewable energy, humidity, temperature, and age to find patterns. The goal is to suggest ways to reduce energy costs and improve energy efficiency.

Methodology¶

a) The energy consumption in buildings was cleaned by checking for null, there were null values present in the renewable energy source and the null values were filled with 'None' and there were no duplicates, dropped unecessary columns and converted categorical values into numerical values using label encoding so it could be analyzed properly.

b) Calculated Mean, Median and Standard Deviation Using Descriptive Statistics.

c) To find the co-relation between the attributes used heatmap.

d) Performed Exploratory Data Analysis where visualizations were generated to analyze study the connection between energy consumption, renewable energy source, insulation types, temperature, sixe and age of the buildings

Key Findings¶

Energy Consumption vs. Building Size¶

Larger buildings tend to consume more energy.

Humidity vs. Insulation Type¶

Buildings with higher humidity tend to have poorer insulation, potentially leading to higher energy usage for temperature control.

Building Size vs. Insulation Type¶

Larger buildings tend to invest in better insulation, reducing overall energy waste.

Building Age vs. Temperature¶

Older buildings generally experience more extreme temperature variations.

Building Age vs. Energy Consumption¶

Older buildings tend to consume more energy, possibly due to outdated insulation and HVAC systems.

Temperature vs. Energy Consumption¶

Higher external temperatures lead to higher energy consumption, mainly due to increased cooling needs.

Recommendations¶

a) Improve Insulation Efficiency by upgrading in smaller buildings to prevent energy loss.

b) Predict Future Energy Consumption Trends.

c) Optimize Temperature Control.

d) Retrofit old buildings with LED lighting, solar panels, and energy-efficient appliances.

e) Improve ventilation.
