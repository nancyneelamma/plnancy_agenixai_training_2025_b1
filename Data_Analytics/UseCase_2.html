REPORT¶

Summary¶

The goal is to understand how factors like attendance, homework, class participation, and family background impact academic performance. By studying this, schools can take steps to help students perform better and score better grades.

Methodology¶

a) The students information was cleaned by checking for null and duplicates, dropped unecessary columns and converted categorical values into numerical values using label encoding so it could be analyzed properly.

b) Calculated Mean, Median and Standard Deviation Using Descriptive Statistics.

c) To find the co-relation between the attributes used heatmap.

d) Performed Exploratory Data Analysis where visualizations were generated to analyze study the connection between grades, attendance, homework, and class participation.

Key Findings¶

Grades & Academic Performance¶

Students with higher grades are more likely to pass.

Age vs Time Spent on homework¶

Students above the age of 20 years old spend maximum time on home works and below age of 18 years old tend to spend slightly more time on homework compared to students of age 18 and 19 years old, possibly due to stricter academic expectations.

Academic Performance vs Time Spent on homework¶

Students who pass generally spend more time on homework compared to failing students.

Class Participation & Attendance¶

Students who participate more in class tend to attend more regularly leads to better engagement and learning and participations is average.

Age & Grades¶

Younger students have a wider range of grades, while older students show more stable performance.

Recommendations¶

a) Encourage Regular Attendance.

b) Provide Personalized Support for Struggling Students.

c) Use Data to Identify At-Risk Students.

d) Use group discussions, interactive activities, and Q&A sessions to make classes more engaging.

e) Help Students from Low-Income Families.
